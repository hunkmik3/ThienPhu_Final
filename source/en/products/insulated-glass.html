Insulated Glass (IGU)

Insulating Glass Units (IGUs), commonly known as double glazing, consist of two or more glass window panes separated by a vacuum or gas-filled space (argon or krypton) to reduce heat transfer across a part of the building envelope. The edges are sealed with a primary and secondary sealant to ensure structural integrity and prevent moisture penetration. IGUs are the standard for energy-efficient construction.

Key Features:

  • Thermal Insulation: Significantly reduces heat loss in winter and heat gain in summer.
  • Energy Efficiency: Lowers energy costs for heating and cooling by 30-50%.
  • Soundproofing: Provides superior acoustic insulation compared to single glazing.
  • Condensation Control: Minimizes moisture buildup on window surfaces.
  • Versatility: Can combine with Low-E, reflective, or tinted glass for enhanced performance.

Applications:

  • Exterior facades of office buildings, hotels, and high-rises.
  • Windows and doors for residential homes.
  • Glass curtain walls.
  • Refrigeration units and commercial freezers.
  • Partition walls in recording studios or meeting rooms.
