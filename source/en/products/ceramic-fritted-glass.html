Ceramic Fritted Glass

Ceramic fritted glass (also known as silk-screened or ceramic printed glass) involves applying ceramic frit (paint) to the glass surface through a screen or digital printer containing a specific design. The glass is then fired in a tempering furnace, fusing the frit to the glass surface. The result is a highly durable, scratch-resistant, and decorative glass product.

Key Features:

  • Exemplary Permanence: The ceramic ink becomes part of the glass; it will not scratch, fade, or peel.
  • Color Variety: Available in a wide range of colors and opacities.
  • Solar Control: Reduces glare and solar heat gain depending on the pattern density.
  • Safety: Is typically tempered or heat-strengthened.
  • Eco-Friendly: Ceramic inks are free of lead and harmful heavy metals.

Applications:

  • Exterior building facades (spandrel glass).
  • Curtain walls, overhead glazing.
  • Interior wall cladding, kitchen backsplashes.
  • Decorative Balustrades.
  • Public art installations.
